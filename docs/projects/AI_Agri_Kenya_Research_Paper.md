# Artificial Intelligence Trends in Agriculture in Kenya: Transforming the Future of Farming

## Abstract

Agriculture is a critical sector in Kenya’s economy, employing a significant portion of the population. The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into agriculture presents opportunities to address challenges such as low productivity, climate change, pest management, and market access. This paper explores the emerging trends of AI applications in Kenyan agriculture, highlighting key innovations, benefits, challenges, and the future outlook of AI-driven transformations. The study synthesizes recent advancements in AI-enabled precision farming, data analytics, remote sensing, and decision support systems tailored to the Kenyan context. It further discusses government initiatives, private sector contributions, and research collaborations fostering the adoption of AI in agriculture. The paper concludes by outlining recommendations for enhancing AI integration to achieve sustainable agricultural growth in Kenya.

**Keywords**: Artificial Intelligence, Agriculture, Kenya, AI Trends, Precision Farming, Food Security, Technology Adoption

## Introduction

Agriculture remains a backbone of Kenya’s economy, contributing approximately 33% to the national Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and providing livelihoods to over 75% of the rural population (World Bank, 2023). Despite its importance, the sector faces persistent challenges including erratic weather patterns, low productivity, pest and disease outbreaks, and limited access to markets and finance. In recent years, the adoption of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies has been gaining momentum as a transformative tool capable of addressing these challenges. AI’s ability to analyze vast data sets, automate complex processes, and provide predictive insights offers a paradigm shift for agricultural practices in Kenya.

This research paper examines the latest trends in AI implementation within the Kenyan agricultural landscape. It reviews applications in precision farming, pest and disease management, weather forecasting, supply chain optimization, and farmer advisory services.
