# African Smart City Readiness Baseline Model

## Methodology

**Indicators (Equal Weight):**
1. **Energy Reliability** - Grid stability, backup systems, renewable integration (0-100)
2. **Fiber Connectivity** - FTTH coverage, internet speed, 5G readiness (0-100)
3. **Digital Infrastructure** - Broadband penetration, cloud services, IoT readiness (0-100)
4. **Urban Density** - Population density affecting smart city rollout efficiency (0-100)

**Data Sources:**
- IEA Energy Access Database
- ITU Digital Development Dashboard
- World Bank Urban Development Data
- African Development Bank Infrastructure Reports
- IMD Smart City Index (regional adaptations)

**Scoring Formula:**
Smart City Score = (Energy Reliability + Fiber Connectivity + Digital Infrastructure + Urban Density) / 4

## Key Findings

**Top 5 Smart City Candidates:**
1. **Kigali, Rwanda** (81.8) - Energy leader with strong digital policy
2. **Cape Town, South Africa** (76.3) - Excellent connectivity, advanced infrastructure
3. **Johannesburg, South Africa** (77.0) - Strong urban ecosystem, good connectivity
4. **Nairobi, Kenya** (75.8) - Tech hub, good infrastructure, moderate density
5. **Cairo, Egypt** (75.5) - High urban density, improving digital infrastructure

**Regional Patterns:**
- East Africa: Kigali leads, Nairobi strong
- Southern Africa: Multiple strong contenders (Cape Town, Johannesburg)
- West Africa: Accra and Lagos show potential but need infrastructure investment
- North Africa: Cairo and Tunis stable bases

**Investment Priority Areas:**
- Lagos: Energy reliability (65) - critical bottleneck
- Kinshasa: All indicators below 60 - requires comprehensive investment
- Dar es Salaam: Fiber connectivity (62) - upgrade path needed
